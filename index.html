Why It's Hard

Rule 219 is a dependency nightmare.

Most Rule 219 exemptions aren't standalone. They depend on other exemptions, which depend on still other conditions. Miss one link in the chain and your "exempt" equipment suddenly requires a permit.

  • 25+ exemptions depend on (d)(2)(C)—the combustion equipment provision. If your heating source doesn't qualify there first, downstream exemptions fail.
  • ~30 provisions trigger Rule 222 filing. The triggers are listed in Rule 222, but whether they apply depends on which Rule 219 exemption you're claiming—and under what conditions.
  • Section (e) can override any (d) exemption. NSPS, NESHAP, ATCM, and toxics rules can force permitting regardless of what (d) says.
  • 8 date-frozen external references to the California Vehicle Code, Health & Safety Code, and CFR—all pinned to April 7, 2023.

This isn't complexity for its own sake. It's 47 years of amendments layered on top of each other. Understanding it requires mapping every cross-reference, carve-back, and nested condition.
